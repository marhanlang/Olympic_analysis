Olympic​ Anal​ysis

Can we predicit if an Olympic Athlete will win a medal based on their BMI?

Project

Using a Neural Network to determine if we can predict medal winners in the Winter and Summer
Olympics based on BMI.

Analysis

The data consisted of 120 years of Olympic Athletes from both the Winter and the Summer Olympics.

The data was found on Kaggle.

We cleaned the data with Excel, Jupyter Notebook and PGAdmin. Cells with missing data and unusable columns were removed.

 The BMI was calculated based on the height and weight of the athletes.


Summarization of Model

The size of our dataset led us to choose a neural network model. The neural network model generally has higher accuracy even with data that has a lot of noise. The hidden layers aid in finding relationships despite noise. This is an advantage when handling large datasets. We have included 2 hidden layers within our model: a relu layer with 8 nodes first and a tanh layer with 5 nodes. Throughout 100 epochs, we observed in increase in loss while accuracy remained stable.
